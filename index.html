Our Menu

Kue Merah Putih

The flavors of Kue Merah Putih can vary, but commonly include a combination of pandan, vanilla, or coconut. The red layers may have a hint of strawberry or raspberry flavoring, while the white layers often carry a subtle coconut aroma. The cake is moist, soft, and has a delicate texture that melts in your mouth.

Kue Pukis

Traditionally cooked in a special mold or cast iron pan, Kue Pukis has a slightly crispy exterior while maintaining a soft and tender interior. The batter used to make Kue Pukis is a combination of flour, eggs, sugar, coconut milk, and sometimes yeast, which gives it a subtle hint of fermentation and adds to its distinct taste.

More Details

Aneka Roti

The aroma of freshly baked Aneka Roti is simply irresistible. The warm, inviting scent of bread fills the air as these delightful treats are taken out of the oven. The bread is typically soft, moist, and fluffy on the inside, with a golden-brown crust that adds a satisfying crunch.

Roti Abon

Roti Abon is a delectable Indonesian snack that combines the rich and savory flavors of shredded meat floss, known as "abon," with soft and fluffy bread. It is a popular choice for breakfast, as a light snack, or as a side dish to accompany meals.

Roti Manis

Roti Manis, which translates to "Sweet Bread" in Indonesian, is a delightful and indulgent treat loved for its soft, fluffy texture and sweet flavors. It is a popular choice for breakfast, as a dessert, or as a snack throughout the day.
